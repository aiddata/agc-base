(So-Financing Agreement between Irish Aid and International Food Policy

As part of Ireland's Bilateral Aid Programme ("Irish Aid"), it has been agreed to provide
€70,000 in financial assistance to the International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI) and
as set out in the enclosed Statement of Work Document.

The substantive interface (including financial) is between Irish Embassy, Lilongwe and
International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI). By this agreement, the International
Food Policy Research Institute, herein referred to as IFPRI, shall be the executing agency. All
documentation and notifications regarding the Technical Assistance for the Logistics Unit (of
Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security) should be sent to the Embassy of Ireland, Private
Bag 8490, Arwa House, Lilongwe 3.

The following terms and conditions governing the making of the payments prevails:

1. IFPRI will ensure that the Technical Assistance for Logistics Unit (of Ministry of
Agriculture and Food Security) is carried out in accordance with the Statement of
Work Document, provided (a copy of which is annexed hereto). IFPRI shall use the
contribution solely for the purposes of the Technical Assistance for Logistics Unit (of
Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security), and will apply it in the manner set out in
the Statement of Work Document. IFPRI should also ensure that Irish Aid support is
appropriately acknowledged in all reports, studies etc. emanating from the Technical
Assistance for Logistics Unit (of Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security).

2. (i) Payments amounting to €70,000 (the Aid Amount) will be provided to IFPRI by Irish
Aid as funding for the period 2010/2011 (10 months) towards the costs incurred in
relation to the Technical Assistance for Logistics Unit (of Ministry of Agriculture and
Food Security) and IFPRI agrees that such monies will be applied in accordance with

the Statement of Work Document (attached). The breakdown of the grant is as

IFPRI shall ensure that all applicable laws, statutes and regulations are complied with

IFPRI shall have sole responsibility for negotiating all appropriate contracts and

Logistics Unit (of Ministry of Agriculture and Food Security). Neither Ireland (the
“State“), nor the Embassy of Ireland shall have or be deemed to have any liability in
relation to any contracts or agreements negotiated or entered into by IFPRI or any
other party in connection with the Technical Assistance for Logistics Unit (of Ministry
of Agriculture and Food Security). Neither the State, nor the Irish Embassy or shall be
deemed for any purpose to be a partner of IFPRI in such contracts.

Without prejudice to the generality of paragraph 5 (i), the parties agree that, save for
the contractual arrangement entered into, no contractual or other relationship shall
exist or be deemed to exist between the State, or Irish Aid and any person employed,
or sent abroad in connection with, or otherwise associated with, or working on the
International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI) unless specific arrangements in
writing are entered into between the Minister or Irish Aid and such person. It is
hereby agreed that none of the personnel working or who shall work on the
International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI) are, nor shall they be deemed to
be, employees or agents of the State or Irish Aid.

IFPRI shall enSUre that all individuals working on the International Food Policy
