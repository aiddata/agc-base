Co—Financing Agreement between Irish Aid and the United Nations Development
Programme (UNDP), Malawi

As part of Ireland's Bilateral Aid Programme ("Irish Aid"), it has been agreed to provide €
110,000 in ﬁnancial assistance, for the One UN Transformation Programme, and as set out in the

enclosed programme document.

The substantive interface (including ﬁnancial) is between Irish Embassy, Lilongwe and UNDP.
By this agreement, the United Nations Resident Coordinator Ofﬁce, herein after referred to as
UNRCO, shall be the executing agency. All documentation and notiﬁcations regarding the One
UN Transformation Programme should be sent to the Embassy of Ireland, Private Bag B490,

Arwa House, Lilongwe 3.

The following terms and conditions governing the making of the payments prevail:

2. (1)

UNRCO will ensure that the One UN Transformation Programme is carried out in
accordance with the terms of the One UN Transformation Programme Document, the
United Nations Business Plan 2008-2009: Uniﬁed UN Country Strategy Malawi,
provided (a copy of which is annexed hereto). UNRCO shall use the contribution
solely for the purposes of the One UN Transformation Programme and will apply it in
the manner set out in the One UN Transformation Programme Document. UNRCO
should also ensure that Irish Aid support is appropriately acknowledged in all reports,
studies etc. emanating from the One UN Transformation Programme.

Payments amounting to € 110,000 (the Aid Amount) will be provided to UNRCO,
through UNDP, by Irish Aid as funding for the period May 2008 to April 2009 towards
the costs incurred by UNRCO in relation to the One UN Transformation Programme
and UNDP agrees that such monies will be applied in accordance with: the One UN
Transformation Programme Proﬁle set out in the One UN Transformation Programme
Document.

Irish Aid shall be entitled to both ﬁnancial and narrative reports from UNDP as
outlined in the One UN Transformation Programme document attached. Irish Aid shall
be entitled to request any additional information or any documentation in respect of
any ﬁnancial or narrative reports before deeming them to be satisfactory.

Any substantial changes to the One UN Transformation Programme shall be agreed in
writing with Irish Aid.

Without prejudice to anything contained in this Agreement, administrative matters
relating to the implementation of this Agreement, including, inter alia, when and how
payments are to be made by Irish Aid to UNDP under this Agreement, shall be as
agreed by the parties from time to time.

Any unused monies provided by Irish Aid will be repaid promptly at the request of
Irish Aid.

The contribution shall be subject exclusively to the internal and external auditing
procedures provided for in the ﬁnancial regulations, rules and directives of UNDP.

UNDP shall ensure that all applicable laws, statutes and regulations are complied with
in the course of implementing the One UN Transformation Programme.

UNDP shall ensure that all policies of insurance necessary or appropriate for an One
UN Transformation Programme of this nature are put in place and UNDP shall ensure
that all premia payable in relation to such insurances are paid when due and that such
insurances shall remain in full force and effect until completion of the One UN
Transformation Programme.

5. (i) UNDP shall have sole responsibility for negotiating all appropriate contracts and
agreements in relation to the implementation of the One UN Transformation
Programme. Neither Ireland (the "State"), the Minister nor Irish Aid shall have or be
deemed to have any liability in relation to any contracts or agreements negotiated or
entered into by UNDP or any other party in connection with the One UN
Transformation Programme. Neither the State, the Minister nor Irish Aid is or shall be
deemed for any purpose to be a partner of UNDP.

(ii) Without prejudice to the generality of paragraph 5 (i), the parties agree that, save for
the contractual arrangement entered into, no contractual or other relationship shall exist
or be deemed to exist between the State, the Minister or Irish Aid and any person
employed, or sent abroad in connection with, or otherwise associated with, or working
on the One UN Transformation Programme unless speciﬁc arrangements in writing are
entered into between the Minister or Irish Aid and such person. It is hereby agreed that
none of the personnel working or who shall work on the One UN Transformation
Programme are, nor shall they be deemed to be, employees or agents of the State, the
Minister or Irish Aid.

(iii) UNDP shall ensure that all individuals working on the One UN Transformation
Programme have appropriate and adequate insurance in relation to their involvement
with the One UN Transformation Programme at the UNDP or their own personal
expense.

6. (i) Although the Minister is making payments under this Agreement towards the cost of
the One UN Transformation Programme, neither the State, the Minister nor Irish Aid
shall bear any liability for any claim of whatever nature or for any court order made
against UNDP or any party, arising from, or in connection with the One UN
Transformation Programme.

(ii) UNDP hereby agrees to indemnify and to keep indemniﬁed the State, the Minister and
Irish Aid against any liabilities, claims, actions or proceedings whatsoever in
connection with the One UN Transformation Programme.

7. UNRCO shall furnish promptly to Irish Aid whenever required to do so by Irish Aid
all such information and documentary evidence as Irish Aid may from time to time
reasonably require in relation to the One UN Transformation Programme and in
particular to vouch compliance by UNDP with any of the terms and conditions of this
Agreement.

8. This Agreement will enter into force on the date stated in Article 2(i) above and,
subject to paragraph 9, shall continue in force until the completion of the One UN
Transformation Programme
