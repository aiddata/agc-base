Co—Financing Agreement between Irish Aid and Malawi Economic Justice
Network (MEJN)

As part of Ireland's Bilateral Aid Programme ("Irish Aid"), it has been agreed to provide
€130,000 in ﬁnancial assistance to the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity
Development, Phase III, and as set out in the enclosed programme document.

The substantive interface (including ﬁnancial) is between Irish Embassy, Lilongwe and
Malawi Economic Justice Network. By this agreement, the Malawi Economic Justice
Network, herein referred to as MEIN, shall be the executing agency. All documentation and
notiﬁcations regarding the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity Development, Phase
III should be sent to the Embassy of Ireland, Private Bag B490, Arwa House, Lilongwe 3.

The following terms and conditions governing the making of the payments prevails:

1. MEJN will ensure that the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity Development,
Phase III is carried out in accordance with the terms of the Project Support Document
and the Accompanying Letter, provided ( copies of which are annexed hereto). MEJN
shall use the contribution solely for the purposes of the Malawi Civil Society
Networks Capacity Development, Phase III, and will apply it in the manner set out
in the Project Support Document and the Accompanying Letter. MEJN should also
ensure that Irish Aid support is appropriately acknowledged in all reports, studies etc.
emanating from the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity Development, Phase
III.

2. (i) Payments amounting to €130,000 (the Aid Amount) will be provided to MEJN, by
Irish Aid as ﬁmding for the period 2009/2010 towards the costs incurred in relation to
the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity Development, Phase III and MEJN
agrees that such monies will be applied in accordance with the Malawi Civil Society
Networks Capacity Development, Phase 3, Project Support Document (attached).

Accordingly, payment of the approved grant of €130,000 will be transferred to MEJN
in December, 2009.

Irish Aid shall be entitled to both ﬁnancial and narrative reports from MEJN relating to
the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity Development, Phase 111, Project
Support Document and Accompanying Letter, attached. Irish Aid shall be entitled to
request any additional information or any relevant documentation in respect of any
ﬁnancial or narrative reports before deeming them to be satisfactory. MEJN shall
adhere to quarterly progress reporting (narrative and ﬁnancial) to Irish Aid. At the end

of the project grant, a ﬁnal narrative and ﬁnancial report should be submitted to Irish
Aid.

Any substantial changes to the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity
Development, Phase III, Project Support Document, shall be agreed in advance and
approved in writing by Irish Aid.

Without prejudice to anything contained in this Agreement, administrative matters
relating to the implementation of this Agreement, including, inter alia, when and how
payments are to be made by Irish Aid to MEJN under this Agreement, shall be as
agreed by the parties from time to time.

Any unused monies provided by Irish Aid will be repaid promptly at the request of
Irish Aid.

MEJN will provide annual audited accounts and management response relating to the
project. Such annual audited accounts shall detail expenditure incurred in respect of the
project. The annual audited accounts shall relate to a calendar year and shall be
submitted to Irish Aid by 31St March on a yearly basis (unless annual audit period is
different and in which case the audited account and management response will be
submitted within 3 months of the audit). Irish Aid shall be entitled to request any
information or any documentation in respect of any such accounts including audit
management letter and responses.

(a) If MEJN is in breach of any of the terms and conditions of this Agreement;

(b) If an order is made or an effective resolution is passed for the winding up of
MEJN or the Malawi Civil Society Networks Capacity Development, Phase
III;

(c) If a receiver is appointed over any of the property of MEJN or if a distress or
execution is levied or served upon any of the property of MEJN and is not paid off
