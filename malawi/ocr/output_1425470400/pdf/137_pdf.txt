Co-Financing Agreement between Irish Aid and the United Nations
Development Programme (UNDP)

As part of Ireland's Bilateral Aid Programme ("Irish Aid"), it has been agreed to provide
€ 500,000 in ﬁnancial assistance to the Civic and Voter Education component of the
‘Support to Electoral Reform and Elections in Malawi Project — Phase 2’, and as set out in
the enclosed programme support document.

The substantive interface (including ﬁnancial) is between Irish Embassy, Lilongwe and United
Nations Development Programme (UNDP). By this agreement, the United Nations
Development Programme, herein after referred to as UNDP, shall be the executing agency. All
documentation and notiﬁcations regarding the Support to Electoral Reform and Elections in
Malawi Project — Phase 2, should be sent to the Embassy of Ireland, Private Bag B490, Arwa
House, Lilongwe 3.

The following terms and conditions governing the making of the payments prevails:

1. UNDP will ensure that the Civic and Voter Education component of the ‘Support
to Electoral Reform and Elections in Malawi Project — Phase 2’ is carried out in
accordance with the terms of the Phase 2 Project Support Document provided (a copy
of which is annexed hereto). UNDP shall use the contribution solely for the purposes of
the Civic and Voter Education component of the ‘Support to Electoral Reform
and Elections in Malawi Project - Phase 2’, and will apply it in the manner set out in
the Phase 2 Project Support Document. UNDP should also ensure that Irish Aid
support is appropriately acknowledged in all reports, studies etc. emanating from the
Support to Electoral Reform and Elections in Malawi Project — Phase 2.

2. (1) Payments amounting to € 500,000 (the Aid Amount) will be provided to UNDP, by
Irish Aid as funding for the period November 2008 — September 2009 towards the
costs incurred in relation to the Civic and Voter Education component of the
‘Support to Electoral Reform and Elections in Malawi Project —- Phase 2’ and
UNDP agrees that such monies will be applied in accordance with the ‘Support to
Electoral Reform and Elections in Malawi — Phase 2’, Project Support Document
(attached). A condition attached to the approval of this grant is that 85 % of the
grant (taking UNDP management costs into account) should be disbursed directly to
